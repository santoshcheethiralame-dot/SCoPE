# DRAGNET — the theory spine

Five results, each with the experiment that measures its premise and the observation that would
refute it. The discipline throughout, learned the hard way: never sell an arithmetic identity as
a discovery — lead with the measured frequency, present the bound as the mechanism behind it.

Placement note: Theorem 5 is the identifiability law originally slated for the third chapter.
It belongs here — it is the formal statement of why the set is the right output object, and the
method paper is stronger with the law than with the remedy alone. The third chapter then goes
all-in on training (self-attribution verified by the oracle), with Theorem 5 as its motivation
rather than its content.

---

## 0. Setup

Fix a question q, a context C = {c_1, …, c_n} of passages, and a deterministic generator M
(greedy decoding, fixed seed). For any subset S ⊆ C write a(S) for the model's answer when
shown exactly S, in the presented order. Answers are compared by the benchmark's equivalence
key ~ (value containment with typed normalization), never string equality.

A case enters the analysis when the full-context answer is wrong: w := a(C), w ≁ gold. Define
the subset game

    v(S) = 1  iff  a(S) ~ w.

- S is **sufficient** if v(S) = 1: the passages in S alone reproduce the wrong value.
- S is **necessary** if v(C \ S) = 0: removing S flips the value.
- 𝔖 denotes the family of **minimal sufficient sets** (no sufficient proper subset); the
  benchmark's leave-one-out "causal" label for a passage c is exactly "{c} is necessary".
- The empty set may be sufficient: then the error is **parametric** — the context explains
  nothing, and no attribution over C is meaningful for it.

Assumptions, stated once and measured rather than assumed silently:

- **(A1) Determinism.** a(·) is a function. Holds by decoding choice; the sampling check
  (greedy vs T = 0.7 agreement 98–100%) bounds what would change without it.
- **(A2) Answer equivalence.** ~ is the benchmark's answer key; its normalization rules are the
  published ones. Rephrase-only changes are not flips by construction.
- **(A3) Support determination.** v is monotone with true points generated by 𝔖:
  v(T) = 1 iff some S ∈ 𝔖 has S ⊆ T. This is exact on the synthetic testbed (by construction)
  and an approximation on live models — adding an irrelevant passage can, rarely, flip an
  answer. The violation rate is measurable: during lattice enumeration, count pairs T ⊂ T'
  with v(T) = 1, v(T') = 0. Report it wherever a result leans on (A3).

Results 1 and 5 lean on (A3); results 3 and 4 do not.

## 1. Theorem 1 — what leave-one-out can and cannot see

**Claim.** Under (A3): a singleton {c} is necessary iff c lies in every member of 𝔖,

    LOO-causal set = ⋂ 𝔖.

In particular, if 𝔖 contains two disjoint members, no passage is LOO-causal: the case is
leave-one-out blind, even though sufficient sets exist and are identifiable.

**Proof.** v(C \ {c}) = 1 iff some S ∈ 𝔖 avoids c (A3). So removal fails to flip exactly when
c misses some member; removal flips for every c in the intersection, and only those. ∎

**Reading.** Leave-one-out does not "miss" redundancy by weakness; it computes the intersection
of the sufficient family, which redundancy makes empty. The benchmark's no-single-culprit rate
is the empirical frequency of ⋂𝔖 failing to be a singleton — a property of the error
distribution, not of any method.

**Ceiling, stated honestly.** Two forms, one definitional and one substantive:
- *Arithmetic:* if the target set R has |R| = m, a single pick covers at most 1/m of it. This
  is bookkeeping, and the paper says so.
- *Substantive:* if 𝔖 contains m members that are observationally exchangeable — there is a
  case symmetry permuting them that leaves every observable a method may use invariant — then
  any equivariant estimator names any designated member with probability at most 1/m. (Proof
  under Theorem 5's symmetry argument.) The designed OR-cells realize the symmetry by
  construction; near-duplicate organic passages realize it approximately.

**Measured by:** roles.jsonl (the causal flags = ⋂𝔖 members), mscs.jsonl (𝔖 itself),
their agreement rate case by case. **Refuted by:** cases where the enumerated ⋂𝔖 disagrees
with the leave-one-out labels beyond the (A3) violation rate.

## 2. Proposition 2 — what search can and cannot promise

**Claim.**
(a) With v given as a circuit/formula, finding a minimum-cardinality sufficient set is NP-hard
    (minimum sufficient reasons for classifiers; inherited, cited, not re-proved).
(b) With v a black box, certifying that a returned sufficient set is *minimum* requires, in the
    worst case, a number of queries exponential in n: an adversary maintaining "some smaller
    hidden AND-set may still exist" is only eliminated by queries that would reveal it, and
    there are C(n, k) candidate k-sets. (Sketch; tie to monotone-function identification lower
    bounds before submission.)
(c) By contrast, a **1-minimal** sufficient set — no single member can be dropped — is
    obtainable in at most 2n + 1 queries given any sufficient superset (grow, then prune one
    pass), and top-down block removal reaches it in O(k log n) when the responsible set is
    small and the priority order is informative.

**Reading.** 1-minimality is not a compromise; it is the strongest guarantee black-box access
admits at polynomial query cost. The extractors' guarantee is optimal in kind, and the paper's
query-budget figure is the empirical face of this proposition.

**Measured by:** the extraction arms' query counts vs set quality (the six-arm table).
**Refuted by:** an extractor that reliably certifies global minimality within o(2^n) queries —
which would be a publishable surprise in itself.

## 3. Theorem 3 — the coverage guarantee (as implemented)

**Claim.** Let each case i carry a nonconformity depth d_i ∈ ℤ_{≥0} ∪ {∞}: the smallest k such
that the top-k of the case's ranking contains a member of its target family (∞ if none does).
If calibration and test cases are exchangeable and τ is the ⌈(n_cal + 1)(1 − α)⌉-th order
statistic of the calibration depths (∞ counted as the largest values), then for a fresh case

    P( d ≤ τ ) ≥ 1 − α.

The singleton family recovers the culprit-rank score, so the benchmark's conformal layer is the
size-1 special case. Mondrian corollary: applied within strata defined by any label-free
function of the case (model identity; a margin bin), the same bound holds conditionally on each
stratum, at that stratum's own n.

**Proof.** Standard split-conformal exchangeability argument over the extended reals; ∞ values
are simply the top of the order and can only enlarge τ — the guarantee is never overstated by
uncoverable calibration mass. ∎

**Reading.** Everything the method promises rides on exchangeability and on the family being
fixed before the ranking is consulted. Orders must be built label-free (presented order, a
method's scores, the sampled surrogate); the abstention decision is made per stratum-bin, never
per test case, so difficulty cannot leak into the promise.

**Measured by:** every coverage-vs-target table; the per-stratum rows. **Refuted by:** test
coverage below target beyond binomial noise — which, if observed under a distribution shift,
is the documented boundary of exchangeability, not a bug to hide.

## 4. Proposition 4 — the coverage–size law (saturation, and the escape)

**Claim.** For any case, the depth against a family 𝔉 satisfies

    d ≥ min_{F ∈ 𝔉} |F|,

since a prefix containing F has length at least |F|. Consequently any prefix rule with coverage
1 − α must have

    τ ≥ Q_{1−α}( min-member size of 𝔉 ),

the (1 − α)-quantile over cases. Two corollaries, one destructive and one constructive:

- **Saturation.** Take 𝔉 = {R}, the whole individually-causal set. Empirically |R| grows with
  retrieval depth (≈ 0.45·k on the natural cells: 2.0 → 3.4 → 4.6 at k = 4 → 8 → 10), so
  Q_{0.9}(|R|) ≈ k and the guaranteed prefix is the whole context at every k — observed exactly
  (τ = k at k = 4, 8, 10). Whole-necessary-set coverage is not expensive by accident; it is
  bounded below by the size of the thing being covered.
- **The escape.** Take 𝔉 = 𝔖, the minimal sufficient family. The bound becomes the quantile of
  the *minimum sufficient set size* — the histogram the enumeration produces. Small minimal
  sufficient sets ⇒ a small-τ guarantee is possible; large ones ⇒ no method, ours included, can
  return small guaranteed sets, and the honest output is abstention. The enumeration therefore
  decides the headline figure before any method is tuned on it.

**Measured by:** the k-sweep saturation (done) and the mscs.jsonl min-size histogram (queued).
**Refuted by:** nothing — it is an inequality; what varies is which side of it the data lands
on, and both sides are findings.

## 5. Theorem 5 — identifiability: when "the responsible set" exists at all

**Claim.** Call an estimand *identifiable* if some estimator is consistent for it from case
observables. Suppose a case admits a symmetry σ — a relabeling of passages that maps 𝔖 to
itself while fixing every observable (content, order statistics, scores of any equivariant
method). If σ moves a member S₁ to a different member S₂, then:

(a) No equivariant estimator of "the responsible set" is consistent: f(x) = S₁ forces
    f(σx) = S₂ on an observationally identical case, so under either ground-truth labeling it
    errs on half the σ-orbit. Single-set attribution is **generically non-identifiable** under
    coalition symmetry.
(b) Every σ-invariant functional of 𝔖 remains identifiable: the family itself, its minimum
    size, the predicate "T contains a member", and coverage statements about calibrated sets.
    In this precise sense, **set-valued output is not a design preference but the only
    consistent estimator shape** under the ambiguity the benchmark measures at 27–53%.

**Proof sketch.** (a) is the two-point symmetry argument above. (b): invariant functionals are
constant on σ-orbits, so orbit-level consistency is unobstructed; the conformal guarantee of
Theorem 3 is such a functional. Formalize σ for the designed cells (decoys are constructed
near-copies: the symmetry is explicit); for organic cells, near-duplicate retrieval passages
realize it approximately, with the approximation error measurable as the observable gap between
the swapped passages. ∎ (to formalize)

**Reading.** This is the law behind the negative result: the 27–53% is the measured frequency
of the regime where (a) bites. It also fixes the target of the third chapter — a model trained
to emit its own sufficient set is being trained to output an identifiable object, which is what
makes oracle-verified training well-posed.

**Measured by:** designed OR cells (exact symmetry), near-duplicate detection on organic cells
(approximate symmetry rate). **Refuted by:** a single-set estimator consistently naming a
designated decoy across relabelings — impossible if the construction is symmetric, so any
apparent success would expose a construction leak, which is itself worth reporting.
